Self-knowledge toolkit

Analysis frameworks

Identify the ontology, taxonomy, and choreography of your personal style through body, color, and aesthetic analysis.

Body type

Observe shoulder-to-hip balance, waist definition, and vertical proportions. Photograph yourself in fitted clothing, draw simple outlines, and compare to silhouettes for hourglass, rectangle, pear, apple, and inverted triangle. Note where you naturally carry volume—this informs your garment structure and fabric weight.

Colour analysis

Evaluate skin tone, undertone, and hair depth beneath natural light. Use white drapes to reveal warm (golden, peach) or cool (rosy, bluish) undertones. Test contrasting swatches to find whether high saturation or soft diffusion best complements you. Map findings to the 12 seasonal palettes and anchor your wardrobe around your dominant chroma, value, and temperature.

Personal style

Catalogue your favorite outfits and rate them by comfort, confidence, and compliment frequency. Track repeated motifs—structures, colors, patterns—and align them with archetypes like Classic, Edgy, Romantic, Minimalist, Bohemian, and Modernist. This analysis becomes your style taxonomy.
