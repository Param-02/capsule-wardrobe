History

The rise of the capsule wardrobe

In the 1970s, London boutique owner Susie Faux coined the term “capsule wardrobe” to describe a streamlined closet of quality essentials. American designer Donna Karan popularized the concept in the 1980s with her Seven Easy Pieces collection. Today, the movement blends sustainability with intentional living, encouraging us to invest in garments that truly earn their place.

Modern capsule wardrobes are less rigid and more expressive. Rather than strict rules, we now focus on curating flexible foundations and layering in personality through accessories and seasonal accents.

Trends

2024 trends worth welcoming

  • Soft tailoring: Relaxed suits in sorbet shades bring ease to workwear.
  • Neo-romance: Rosettes, ribbon ties, and sheer layers add drama without bulk.
  • Earthcore: Artisan knits, hammered metals, and earthy dyes embrace slow craft.
  • Technicolour neutrals: Chocolate, charcoal, and midnight navy act as new-season bases.

Integrate trends gradually — one statement piece mixed with your core staples keeps outfits wearable.

Essay

Why fewer clothes mean more creativity

Limiting your closet encourages experimentation. When options are streamlined, we spend less time choosing and more time styling. Capsule dressing teaches us to remix silhouettes, layer textures, and personalize outfits with accessories.

“Constraints can be the catalyst for your most expressive outfits. Curate intentionally and your style voice gets louder.”

Try a 10x10 challenge: ten items styled into ten outfits. Document the process to see which combinations excite you the most.

Minimalist wardrobe

How to reset your closet in one weekend

  1. Edit: Pull everything out and sort into love, tailor, donate, and store.
  2. Assess: Identify gaps across categories — outerwear, tops, bottoms, footwear.
  3. Plan: Use our capsule builder to select replacements and wish-list items.
  4. Style: Create flat lays of five go-to outfits for the week ahead.

Set a timer for each step to stay focused. The goal is clarity, not perfection.

Capsule math

Outfit equations that work

Use these mix-and-match formulas to generate outfits fast:

  • Column base + Third piece + Signature accessory
  • Statement bottom + Neutral knit + Sleek shoe
  • Hero dress + Layering knit + Boot or sneaker
  • Cozy knit + Structured bottom + Elevated earring

Save your favourite combinations as phone notes for easy reference before you head out the door.

Interview

5 questions with a capsule convert

We sat down with creative director Lila Moreno to chat about her minimalist closet. Her number one tip? “Define your palette and stick to it. I only buy pieces that play nicely together.”

Lila’s daily uniform rotates between tailored trousers, silk blouses, and statement earrings. She keeps a small rail of seasonal accents to refresh outfits without overwhelming her wardrobe.
